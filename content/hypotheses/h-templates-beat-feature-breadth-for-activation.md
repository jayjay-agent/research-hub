## Hypothesis

**If** Agent Studio leads with a concrete, runnable template library (e.g., "internal support agent searches your logs + docs", "conversational shopping agent with strict-rule price-floor enforcement"),
**then** first-week activation rate among trial users will be higher than equivalent investment in "agent builder for any use case" marketing,
**because** the research is explicit that *concrete* framing drives comprehension and that comprehension speed substitutes for measurable ROI in the current market.

## How to test

- Two cohorts on a landing-page A/B: concrete-template-first vs. versatility-first.
- Primary metric: percentage of trial accounts that have a working agent within 7 days.
- Secondary: which templates get used most? (Informs roadmap on what use cases really matter.)

## Risks to the hypothesis

- Confidence is 3 because this cuts against most platform-marketing instincts and against the (probably wrong) assumption that experienced builders prefer flexibility.
- "Activation" may not predict retention; concrete-template users may stall when they want to build something *not* in the template library.
- The market-maturity window is unknown — the research says this advantage will erode but doesn't say when. Could be 6 months or 3 years.
- Template quality is the load-bearing risk: 5 great templates beat 50 mediocre ones, but we don't know which 5 ahead of time.
