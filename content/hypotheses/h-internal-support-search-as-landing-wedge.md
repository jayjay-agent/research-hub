## Hypothesis

**If** Agent Studio's first GTM motion centers on internal support search (multi-doc + log retrieval for customer-support and engineering teams),
**then** initial design-partner activation will be faster and retention will be higher than landing with the customer-facing conversational-search dream,
**because** internal use cases are what builders are actually shipping today, have lower brand-risk gates, and produce the "freed up the team" ROI signal that's the only durable proof customers currently accept.

## How to test

- Recruit a 3-5 design-partner cohort specifically for internal support search use cases.
- Compare time-to-production-deployment, retention at 90 days, and expansion-to-other-workflows against a comparable cohort landed with customer-facing conversational shopping use cases.
- Secondary: do internal-support landers eventually graduate to customer-facing? If yes, the wedge expands; if no, we may be in the wrong market.

## Risks to the hypothesis

- Confidence is 3 — this is a real strategic choice that goes against Algolia's brand center of gravity (customer-facing search).
- Internal-support search market is contested by Glean, Rovo, Notion AI, and platform-native AI. We may not have a structural advantage there.
- "Land internal, expand to customer-facing" assumes expansion is easy. The two markets have different buyers, different SLAs, different brand profiles — expansion may stall.
- Customer-facing dreams from research #1 customers may pull us back to the original positioning whether we want it or not.
