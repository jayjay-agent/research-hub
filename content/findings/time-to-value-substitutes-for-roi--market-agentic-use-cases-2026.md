## Finding

Measurable ROI on agent platforms is hard for the same structural reasons it's hard in the broader automation field: cleared-out time gets filled with new work, attribution is messy, and the platform itself often doesn't expose direct metrics. **The dominant value signal is anecdotal time-saved.**

But the lack of clean ROI doesn't block investment, because three factors mitigate it:

1. **Bundled tooling** — when agent-building is included in an existing license, there's no new investment to justify.
2. **Strong templates / frameworks** that drastically reduce setup time.
3. **Concrete (not open-ended) framing** — templates that show specifically what the agent is for, vs. language that emphasizes "imaginative versatility."

This trio adds up to a **time-to-value wedge**: if you can make a builder *understand* the value within their first hour, you buy temporary permission for the ROI question to stay fuzzy.

The report is explicit that this is a **time-bounded competitive advantage**. As the market matures, builders will demand measurable ROI and the wedge will narrow.

## Mechanism (hypothesized)

Two effects compound:

1. **Comprehension is a leading indicator of investment.** If a builder can see exactly what the agent does in their context (via a concrete template) before they finish reading the marketing, they've already self-qualified. Open-ended "you could build anything" framing makes them do the self-qualification work, which most won't.
2. **Bundling collapses the buy decision.** "No new investment" eliminates procurement friction entirely — even if the agent only saves 10 minutes a week, that's a positive ROI against zero cost.

The combination explains a market reality the report names indirectly: ChatGPT-as-bundled-LLM (research #1) and "agent tools in existing licenses" (this source) win on the *same* mechanism — they remove the buy decision from the equation.

## Counter-evidence to look for

- The "concrete framing > imaginative versatility" claim cuts against most platform marketing instincts (developers love versatility). Worth testing whether the inverse is true for highly experienced builders.
- The mitigators may not stack — bundling alone may already eliminate the need for templates.
- The report says the time-to-value wedge will close as the market matures, but doesn't say when. Could be 6 months or 3 years; matters a lot for roadmap pacing.
