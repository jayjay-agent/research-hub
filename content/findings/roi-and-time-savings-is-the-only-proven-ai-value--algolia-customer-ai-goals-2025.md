## Finding

Customers report a **structural ROI-proof problem** for AI investments: customer-facing AI changes are hard to attribute, A/B testing infrastructure is often missing, and the only AI outcome they treat as confidently provable is **internal team time-savings**. New AI tools get longer trial periods and deeper scrutiny than non-AI tools.

This compounds with the ChatGPT-incumbency finding: if "freed up the content team" is the only durable ROI signal, ChatGPT delivers it for $0 marginal cost.

## Mechanism (hypothesized)

Three feedback loops:

1. **Attribution opacity.** AI-driven personalization changes touch ranking, copy, and UI simultaneously; isolating the AI contribution requires A/B infrastructure most of the sample doesn't have.
2. **Lengthened trials.** Scrutiny pushes evaluation timelines past the window where the AI feature would have proven itself.
3. **The "freed up team" pattern.** This ROI is visible week-one, doesn't need attribution math, and lands easily in leadership reviews.

The strategic consequence for Agent Studio: any AI product that requires customer-side A/B testing to demonstrate value will lose to ChatGPT on time-to-proof. Products that *deliver visible team-time-savings as a side effect* of their primary function will clear the trial gate faster.

## Counter-evidence to look for

- Severe market pressure may overwhelm the ROI-proof problem before this finding ages — the report itself flags this as in flux.
- Customers with mature experimentation platforms (likely larger or more sophisticated than this sample) may not face the attribution problem the same way.
- "Time savings" satisfaction may decay as the novelty wears off; need to see whether retention of these tools holds past year one.
