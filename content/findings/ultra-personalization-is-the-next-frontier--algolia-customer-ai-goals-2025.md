## Finding

Across the sample, ultra-personalization is the highest-priority area of *planned* investment for the next year — not just a stated wish. Customers are already capturing more 1P data, and several are evaluating competitor Perso vendors (DynamicYield was named specifically for its experimentation capabilities). The framing has shifted: "relevance" itself is being redefined as personalization, not as keyword match quality.

Crucially, the ambition isn't segment-level Perso. Customers want **1:1**, with effects that go beyond ranking into **UI mutation** — the report's example is a beauty retailer surfacing "buy now" buttons in PLPs for an identified impulse shopper while suppressing irrelevant hair-color SKUs.

## Mechanism (hypothesized)

Two pressures converge:

1. **Diminishing returns on keyword tuning.** After years of Algolia investment, customers see flattening lift from yet-more-rules and yet-more-custom-ranking — so the next marginal dollar moves to a new lever.
2. **Visible competitor pressure.** Sites the customers shop on themselves have started doing this, and the contrast is becoming a brand expense.

The result is a structural ask for **per-user ranking strategy selection** rather than per-segment. Today's workaround — proliferating rules and indices to approximate fine-grained segments — actively makes the codebase worse (rules fight custom ranking) and doesn't reach the dream state.

## Counter-evidence to look for

- Only **one** company in the sample seemed ready to do the foundational backend work to actually deliver 1:1 personalization. The other 9 talk the dream but ship piecemeal. A wide-scale survey may confirm or refute that gap.
- "1:1" may be overclaimed — what customers can actually act on may stop at segment-of-one-thousand, not literal individual.
- Privacy-regulation tightening (EU, US state laws) may push the ceiling down before customers can build the data backbone.
