## Finding

When asked which benefit they'd most want from an agentic platform, 41% picked **agent analytics and tuning tools optimized for their specific industry** — the single clearest top choice. The split on a related question between "analytics optimized for search/discovery metrics" (43%) and "analytics optimized for industry-relevant metrics like add-to-cart" (39%) signals that customers want analytics customization, not a single fixed lens.

The research author flagged a strategic split inside this: customers who prefer industry-relevant metrics map closer to **Agent Studio** (search assistant, B2C, purchase-physical-good segments); customers who prefer search/discovery metrics map closer to **Ask AI** (conversational, B2B, read/learn + content, stream/experience segments).

## Mechanism (hypothesized)

"Search analytics" is well-understood and table-stakes for Algolia customers — CTR, conversion through search, zero-result rate. "Agent analytics" doesn't have an established vocabulary yet. Customers don't know what numbers to ask for, but they know they need their *business* numbers (add-to-cart, GMV, completion rate) to attach to the agent's behavior, not generic chat-completion stats from an LLM provider. The "industry-optimized" framing is shorthand for "speak my KPIs, not the model's."

## Counter-evidence to look for

- A general-purpose analytics surface that lets customers define their own metrics could neutralize the "industry-optimized" preference — the underlying need may be customization, not pre-fab industry templates.
- The Analytics Assistant segment (19%) treats analytics as table-stakes (per past research) and is least willing to pay more for it. Industry-optimized analytics may not be a willingness-to-pay driver even if it's a desire driver.
- Strong demand for "industry-optimized" could decompose into industry-specific *templates* rather than industry-specific *primitives*, in which case the build surface (not the analytics surface) is doing the work.
