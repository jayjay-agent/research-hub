## TL;DR

- **10 enterprise customers (mostly eCommerce tech leadership)** were unusually consistent: their dream AI is **conversational search powered by 1:1 ultra-personalization**, fed by data they can't currently get into Algolia (CDP, CMS, CRM, training manuals).
- They explicitly **don't want monolithic AI products**. They want **orchestratable, agent-callable functions** — explicitly framed as "multi-agentic" and aligned with MCP. The report's core recommendation echoes this.
- **ChatGPT is the silent competitor.** 9 of 10 already pay for it. Any new AI tool gets killed if customers can imitate it in ChatGPT — even when ChatGPT performs worse, because the license is sunk cost and the time savings are real.
- The #2 ask is **a natural-language analytics + tuning assistant** that can interrogate performance ("How did our purple colorway perform?") and propose config changes with reasoning.
- **Ranking and personalization logic isn't programmatically pliable enough** today — customers proliferate Rules and indices as a workaround, which then fight each other.

## Why it matters for Agent Studio

This is direct strategic input for Agent Studio's bet. The customer language — "multi-agentic," "agentically first," "engines talking to each other on the backend" — *is* the Agent Studio premise stated back at us by the people who'd buy it. The report's explicit recommendation is to "invest strategically in MCP and multi-agentic frameworks" rather than ship the dream products as integrated suites. That's an Agent Studio mandate, not a Search or Perso mandate.

The corollary is uncomfortable: Agent Studio's value prop has to clear a **higher bar than ChatGPT-can't-do-this**, because ChatGPT incumbency is actively crowding out targeted AI purchases. The differentiator has to be the *orchestrated, data-grounded, ranking-aware* layer that ChatGPT structurally can't replicate against a customer's catalog and analytics.

## What's still open

- This is the *qualitative* phase. A wide-scale survey is planned as a follow-up — quantitative weighting of which dreams are most common is still TBD.
- Sample skews enterprise eCommerce. SaaS/content/B2B representation is thin; we can't yet say whether the orchestration-vs-monolith preference holds outside retail.
- Customers describe what they'd buy, not what they'd actually deploy. The report itself flags the gap: real recent explorations are "piecemeal, oriented around time-savings" — not the comprehensive dream solutions.
- We don't know how the ChatGPT-incumbency dynamic shifts as enterprise OpenAI products mature (custom GPTs, file uploads, native MCP support).
- Open question on whether the "image visualization personalizer" idea — changing result imagery per individual — is a real demand pattern or one vivid example that interview prompting amplified.
