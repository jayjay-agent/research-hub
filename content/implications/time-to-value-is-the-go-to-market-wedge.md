## What the research implies

[[time-to-value-substitutes-for-roi--market-agentic-use-cases-2026]] identifies a temporary but real market dynamic: builders can't measure ROI cleanly, so they invest based on **speed to comprehending value**. Three named mitigators:

1. Bundled into existing licenses (zero new investment friction)
2. Strong templates / frameworks (faster setup)
3. Concrete-not-versatile framing (faster comprehension)

The same mechanism that lets ChatGPT win on incumbency ([[chatgpt-incumbency-crowds-out-targeted-ai--algolia-customer-ai-goals-2025]]) is the mechanism Agent Studio has to compete on if it's going to land at all. Concrete templates that demonstrate "this *exact* use case" beats marketing that says "you could build anything."

The window is bounded. The report says explicitly: "as this market matures, we should not expect this to hold true." The bet is to use the time-to-value wedge to land design partners, build trust, and *then* invest in measurable-ROI infrastructure as the market demands it.

## Concrete implications

1. **Ship a template library before the platform polish.** Templates have to be specific, real, and end-to-end runnable — not "examples of what you could build." Match the dominant workflow patterns (ticket → categorize → solve → push), the internal-support-search use case, and the customer-facing conversational shopping use case from research #1.
2. **Distribute through bundling, not standalone purchase.** Agent Studio capability available inside an existing Algolia plan kills the procurement decision. The same mechanism that lets ChatGPT win works for us if we're already in the contract.
3. **Marketing copy: concrete > versatile.** "Build an internal support agent that searches your logs and docs" lands; "build any agent for any use case" loses. This cuts against most platform-marketing instincts.
4. **Build measurable-ROI infrastructure in parallel — but quietly.** The market maturity transition will be abrupt when it comes. The product needs the metrics surface ready *before* the market demands it; we just don't lead with it.
5. **Treat first-hour activation as a top-level product metric.** From the moment a builder lands on the template library to "agent doing something useful against my data" — every minute of latency costs adoption.

## What's still unknown

- The pacing: how long does the time-to-value wedge last? Could be 6 months or 3 years; the answer changes roadmap aggression.
- Whether the templates need to be Algolia-built or whether community templates can carry the weight. Zapier's template library is mostly community; we don't know if that pattern fits.
- How well "bundled into existing licenses" works when Algolia's existing plans aren't shaped around agent usage. May require Agent Studio to be a free included surface for some plan tiers.
- The interaction with the strict-rules wedge: do templates need to come pre-loaded with strict-rule examples, or does that come in a second product pass?
